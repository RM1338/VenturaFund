Our Story

I'm Robert Chen, and I'm urgently seeking help for my wife, Michelle, who suffered a sudden and life-threatening heart attack three weeks ago.

Michelle is only 45 years old with no prior heart issues. She's a certified yoga instructor who has always lived a healthy, active lifestyle‚Äîteaching classes five days a week, eating well, and maintaining excellent physical fitness. The heart attack came completely out of nowhere and shattered our sense of security.

On October 23rd, Michelle collapsed during her morning routine. I called 911 immediately, and paramedics rushed her to Northwestern Memorial Hospital. After emergency stabilization and extensive testing, we received devastating news: the heart attack caused significant damage to her heart, affecting multiple arteries.

The Critical Situation

The cardiac team determined that Michelle needs immediate open-heart surgery with a triple bypass. Without this operation, her cardiologist says she faces a high risk of another, potentially fatal heart attack within months. The surgery must be performed by a specialized team at Northwestern's Advanced Cardiac Care Unit.

The medical costs are overwhelming. While we have insurance, it doesn't come close to covering everything. The total out-of-pocket expense for surgery, intensive care, cardiac rehabilitation, and necessary medications is $60,000‚Äîmoney we simply don't have.

Michelle has dedicated her life to helping others find peace, strength, and wellness through yoga. She's taught pregnant women, cancer survivors, seniors, and people recovering from injuries. She's touched hundreds of lives with her kindness and compassion.
