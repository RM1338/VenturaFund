Our Story

My name is Emma Wilson, and I'm raising funds for my father, David Wilson, who was recently diagnosed with stage 3 colon cancer.

Dad is 58 years old and has always been the rock of our family‚Äîa devoted husband, loving father of three, and dedicated high school basketball coach for over 30 years. He's the kind of person who never complains, always puts others first, and has touched countless lives through his mentorship of young athletes.

Two months ago, Dad started experiencing severe abdominal pain and unexplained weight loss. After weeks of tests and doctor visits, we received the devastating diagnosis: stage 3 colon cancer that has spread to nearby lymph nodes.

The Medical Journey

The oncologist has recommended an aggressive treatment plan that includes:

  • Immediate surgery to remove the tumor and affected lymph nodes
  • Six months of chemotherapy
  • Radiation therapy as needed
  • Regular PET scans and follow-up care

While Dad has health insurance through the school district, the out-of-pocket costs are staggering. We're facing $50,000 in medical expenses that aren't covered, including specialized cancer medications, multiple hospital stays, and ongoing treatment costs.

How Your Support Helps

Every dollar raised will go directly toward Dad's cancer treatment:

  • Surgery and hospital stay costs
  • Chemotherapy medications and infusions
  • Radiation therapy sessions
  • Follow-up scans and appointments
  • Travel expenses for treatments at Dana-Farber Cancer Institute

Dad has spent three decades coaching young people, teaching them about teamwork, perseverance, and never giving up. He's been there for so many families during their toughest times. Now, our family needs your support as we fight this battle together.

We're 82% of the way to our goal! Your donation, no matter the size, brings us closer to ensuring Dad gets the treatment he needs to beat this cancer and return to the basketball court he loves.

Thank you for your kindness, prayers, and support. We'll continue updating this page with Dad's progress throughout his treatment journey.
