Our Story

Hi, I'm John Martinez, and I'm reaching out on behalf of my sister, Sarah. Our family is facing an unexpected medical crisis, and we need your help to save Sarah's life.

Sarah is a 32-year-old elementary school teacher who has dedicated her life to helping children learn and grow. She's always been the healthiest person I know‚Äîshe runs marathons, eats well, and has never had a serious health issue until now.

Three weeks ago, Sarah collapsed during a routine morning jog. After rushing her to the emergency room, we received devastating news: she has a rare cardiac condition that requires immediate surgery. Without this operation, doctors say she has less than 6 months to live.

The Medical Situation

Sarah needs a complex cardiac procedure that will repair a congenital defect that went undetected for years. The surgery must be performed by a specialized team at UCLA Medical Center, one of only a handful of hospitals in the country equipped to handle this rare condition.

The total cost of the surgery, hospital stay, and recovery is $75,000. While Sarah has insurance, it only covers a fraction of the costs, leaving our family with an overwhelming medical bill that we simply cannot afford.

How Your Support Will Help

Every dollar raised will go directly toward Sarah's medical expenses, including:

  • Pre-surgery consultations and diagnostic tests
  • The surgical procedure itself
  • 7-10 day hospital stay and intensive care
  • Post-operative care and rehabilitation
  • Medications and follow-up appointments

Sarah has touched so many lives through her work as a teacher. She's that person who stays late to help struggling students, who uses her own money to buy classroom supplies, and who makes every child feel valued and important. Now, it's our turn to help her.

We're asking for your support to give Sarah the chance to continue doing what she loves‚Äîteaching and inspiring the next generation. No donation is too small, and every contribution brings us closer to saving her life.

Thank you for taking the time to read Sarah's story and for any support you can provide. We'll keep this page updated with her progress.
