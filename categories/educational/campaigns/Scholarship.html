Our Story

My name is Priya Sharma, and I am the proud daughter of Indian immigrants who sacrificed everything to give me a better life. I'm raising funds for my college scholarship so I can become the first in my family to attend university.

My parents moved to the U.S. with little more than hope and determination. My father works two jobs‚Äîdays as a mechanic and nights as a rideshare driver. My mother works in a factory and tutors children on weekends. They taught me to work hard, dream big, and value education above all else.

This spring, I was accepted to Georgia Tech's Computer Science program‚Äîmy dream school! I also received several smaller scholarships, but I still face a $20,000 gap to cover my first year of tuition, housing, books, and required laptop. My parents cannot afford to help, and I don't want to burden them with loans they cannot repay.

Why This Matters

First-generation college students often face unique hurdles. Many of my friends gave up on their dreams because of financial stress. I don't want to let that happen to me, but I can't do it alone.

Computer science is my passion. I've volunteered teaching girls to code, built apps for my community, and participated in hackathons. I want to use technology to solve real problems and inspire others with my story.

How the Funds Will Be Used

  • Tuition for Georgia Tech ($10,000/semester)
  • Campus housing and meal plan
  • Required laptop/software for CS coursework
  • Books, supplies, and transportation

We've raised 88% of our goal! Your support will allow me to start college this fall and break the cycle for future generations. Every dollar truly matters.

Thank you for investing in my future. I will pay this forward by helping other young women in STEM. Please help me become the first in my family to go to college!
