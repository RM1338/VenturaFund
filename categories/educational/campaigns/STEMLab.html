STEM Lab for Underserved School

D
David Park
Detroit, Michigan

Our Story

My name is David Park, and I'm a high school science teacher at Jefferson High School in Detroit. I'm raising funds to build a state-of-the-art STEM lab that will transform how our students learn science, technology, engineering, and mathematics.

Jefferson High serves 850 students from low-income families in one of Detroit's most underserved neighborhoods. While schools in wealthy suburbs have cutting-edge technology labs, our students are learning STEM subjects with outdated textbooks and virtually no hands-on equipment.

I've been teaching here for 12 years, and I've watched brilliant, curious students fall behind simply because they don't have access to the same resources as their peers. Many have incredible potential in fields like robotics, computer science, and engineering‚Äîbut they've never even touched a circuit board or written a line of code.

The Opportunity Gap

In today's world, STEM literacy is essential for future success. But the opportunity gap is widening. Students at well-funded schools learn coding in elementary school, build robots in middle school, and have access to 3D printers and advanced software in high school. Our students deserve the same opportunities.

Last year, one of my students, Marcus, taught himself coding using his phone and free YouTube videos. When I asked what he wanted to study in college, he said, "I want to be a software engineer, but I don't think schools like MIT would want someone like me." His words broke my heart‚Äînot because of his circumstances, but because of his immense talent being overlooked.

The STEM Lab Vision

We're building a comprehensive STEM lab that will include:

  • 20 Modern Computers ($15,000): For coding, CAD design, and research
  • Robotics Equipment ($12,000): LEGO Mindstorms, Arduino kits, and VEX robotics systems
  • 3D Printers ($8,000): Three professional-grade 3D printers for engineering projects
  • Science Equipment ($7,000): Microscopes, lab supplies, and experiment kits
  • Maker Space Tools ($5,000): Electronics workbenches, soldering stations, and prototyping supplies
  • Software & Licensing ($3,000): Professional software for CAD, programming, and design

The Impact

This STEM lab will serve over 400 students annually and will:

  • Provide hands-on learning in coding, robotics, and engineering
  • Prepare students for high-paying STEM careers
  • Level the playing field with suburban schools
  • Host after-school STEM clubs and weekend workshops
  • Partner with local colleges for mentorship programs
  • Increase college enrollment in STEM majors

Our students are just as smart, talented, and hardworking as students anywhere else. They just need the tools and opportunities. With your support, we can give them both.

We've raised $29,000 of our $50,000 goal‚Äî58% funded! Every donation brings us closer to opening this lab and changing the trajectory of hundreds of students' lives. Please help us build a brighter future for Detroit's youth.

Thank you for investing in these incredible students. Together, we're not just building a lab‚Äîwe're building careers, dreams, and hope.
